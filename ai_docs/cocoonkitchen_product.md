# CocoonKitchen — Product Document

## Why (the strategy)

Most recipe tools count calories and macros. Almost none answer the question a health-conscious cook actually asks at the moment of choosing: **"how processed is this, really — and what's the one-ingredient fix?"** CocoonKitchen scores a recipe's ingredient quality (ultra-processing, added sugar, fat quality, sodium/preservatives, whole-food ratio, additives), names the offenders, and offers three practical swaps. The stance is a chef's and a physician's at once: food should be delicious first, whole second, and never moralized — we raise awareness of processing, we don't shame plates or give medical advice.

**The real product bet:** the moat is not the UI, it's a scoring rubric that agrees with an expert human. That's why the eval harness and golden set are first-class citizens from day one, and why no feature ships unless an eval number earns it.

## What (v0 scope)

Paste a recipe or a link → get a Verdict card: 0–100 score, band (Clean / Mostly Clean / Processed / Ultra-processed), sub-scores, flagged ingredients, 3 swaps.

**Definition of done for v0:** a shareable Streamlit URL where pasting a recipe returns a sane card; every input/output logged; `evaluate.py` runs cleanly and prints band accuracy + score MAE on sample rows.

## Non-goals / tripwires (do NOT build)
Vector DB, embeddings, RAG, accounts/auth, real database, shopping lists, meal planning, scraper edge-case heroics (paste is the fallback). Each must later earn its place by moving an eval number.

| 12 | Clean-recipe swap UX — don't force swaps on an already-clean dish (**parked 2026-07-19, NOT approved**) | The output contract requires **exactly 3 swaps**, so for an already-clean recipe the model fills the quota with hollow `from_ingredient: "none"` pseudo-swaps (Amber's manual test, 2026-07-19: salmon + quinoa + kale → "Nothing flagged," then three `none → salmon fillet / olive oil / lemon juice` entries under **"Swaps to try"**). ### Safety & Ethics roadmap additions (proposed 2026-07-20, NOT approved — see `ai_docs/safety.md`)

Prompted by the Safety & Ethics interview playbook. These make CocoonKitchen's **harm-class** surface explicit —
our entire pitfalls catalog to date is quality-class, and the only *enforced* refusal is `is_recipe:false`. All rows
are human-owned to sequence; the `safety.md` register is the source of truth and holds the SHIR sizing + open decisions.

| Phase | Name | Delivers | Gate to exit |
|-------|------|----------|--------------|
| 5 (amend) | Deploy safety hardening | Disclaimer/scope **always** renders (advisory, not medical — Air Canada framing); a lightweight **output filter** for medical/nutrition *claims* (H2); **prompt-injection hardening** for pasted + fetched content, keeping schema-validation as the firewall (H7); a shipped **`SAFETY.md` / model card** (intended use, limits, refusal scope, model+rubric provenance — H9). | Public surface never leaks internals; medical-claim filter + injection hardening covered by tests; model card shipped; `/security-review` clean. |
| 6 (amend) | Safety & ethics eval slices | Negative/harm evals on the golden harness (labels human-owned, I-draft-you-curate): **never-emits-medical-claim** (H2); **unsafe-swap** — does a swap introduce a common allergen / violate a stated restriction (H1); **cuisine-bias** slice — per-cuisine band accuracy + mean signed error (H6); **injection red-team** slice (H7). | Each slice runs in `evaluate.py`; harm-class slices report a pass/fail number, not just band accuracy; no regression to the core golden set. |

## User stories

### Functional (v0 — being built)
- As a home cook, I paste recipe text and get a score, band, and flagged ingredients in one card.
- As a home cook, I paste a recipe URL and it parses automatically (paste as fallback when scraping fails).
- As a home cook, I get 3 practical, non-shaming swaps with one-line reasons.
- As the product owner, every scored recipe is logged (input + verdict) so I can audit behavior.
- As the product owner, I can run the eval harness against the golden set and see band accuracy, score MAE, and per-component error.
- As the product owner, I can open an **observability & labeling console** to (a) author golden rows from a recipe directly and (b) correct/label real logged verdicts into golden rows + swap-quality grades, exporting to `golden_set.csv` — this is how I build the 20–50-row golden set the evals need (Phase 4).

### Next up (post-v0, each must earn its place)
- As a home cook, I want to see the whole recipe in context on the card (its title and full ingredient list, not just the flagged items) and understand *why* each ingredient was flagged and how each sub-score was reached — so I can trust the number instead of taking it on faith. (Phase 7 — explainability & trust; depends on a validated rubric from Phase 6.)
- As a home cook, when an ingredient is flagged I want to see a *ladder* of cleaner alternatives — not just one — each with its trade-off (more time, more effort, some flavor change), so I decide how far to go. (Phase 8 — "cleaner spectrum"; needs a brainstorming session to scope before it's built. Validated by the 2026-07 labeling feedback: pasta ladder = homemade → veggie noodles → bean/egg noodles; the golden-draft "other alternatives" notes are the first data slice of this.)
- As the labeler/product owner, I want each ingredient rendered as an accordion table row — with a column showing whether it has its own graded score or only the recipe-level score exists — that expands to the ingredient's details (flag rationale, item-of-concern, alternatives). (Phase 7; from the 2026-07-14 labeling retro.)
- As a shopper, I want to paste (or scan) a **store-bought product's** ingredient label and see how "clean" it is — score, band, and the additives dragging it down — so I can judge packaged foods, not just recipes. (Phase 10 — parked idea, gated on a validated Phase-6 rubric.)
- As a home cook, I want to hand the app a recipe or a packaged product and get back a **cleaner from-scratch version** of it — the whole clean rebuild, not just individual swaps. (Phase 11 — parked idea, builds on Phase 8.)
- As a home cook, when my recipe is already clean I want the card to *celebrate* that — not hand me three hollow "swaps" for nothing — and to offer any extras as clearly-optional flourishes, not corrections. (Phase 12 — parked idea; from Amber's 2026-07-19 manual test.)
- Rubric tuning loop with regression tracking across prompt versions.
- LLM-as-judge for swap quality (replaces manual 1–5 grading).
- Knowledge base for additive/ingredient facts (CSV/SQLite) — only if eval errors localize to ingredient knowledge.
- Prettier front end (FastAPI/Next.js) — only when someone other than us uses it enough to care.
